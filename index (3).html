About

Curriculum at the intersection of climate, community, and careers

I'm a curriculum designer at Generation, where my recent work transitioning and upskilling workers into climate careers has spanned every stage of the design process: research, scoping and sequencing, establishing aligned learning objectives and assessments, designing a blend of self-paced, live virtual, and in-person workshop content, and iterating based on learner feedback and industry input.

Working with subject matter experts like the Retrofit Academy, local government, and active retrofit contractors, I developed a comprehensive Retrofit Advisor course covering current government retrofit schemes, the realities of the climate crisis, and the role of inefficient home heating in fuel poverty. It was designed to prepare participants for accreditation exams, but more importantly for the on-the-job realities of supporting homeowners through the transition to more energy-efficient homes. Over 85% of the initial cohort were employed with green jobs employers within three months of graduation.

Throughout my career in education, one theme has become increasingly clear: learning is most effective when part of a vibrant, delightful, community-centered experience. I love to take dense, technical material and bring it to life, and to use technology as a tool that amplifies but never replaces the magic of a great teacher.

I also direct London Green Wood, a greenwood carving cooperative in East London, where I facilitate monthly community sessions for mixed-ability groups and oversee curriculum for seven courses reaching 700+ participants a year. Outside work I'm a climber, trail runner, and swimrun athlete drawn to wild places and the natural world. Through my own teaching fellowship at the University of Notre Dame, I saw firsthand what a cohort of people on a shared mission can do for a career, and it shaped how I think about learning, community, and what a fellowship can do.

Challenge

Millions of UK homes need energy-efficiency retrofitting, creating urgent demand for Retrofit Advisors, a role with no established training curriculum. The program needed to meet industry certification standards while preparing diverse adult learners for immediate employment.

Approach

  • Built the full curriculum from scratch: research, scoping, sequencing, objectives, and assessments
  • Collaborated with experts including the Retrofit Academy, local government, and active contractors
  • Integrated technical content, soft skills, and community-based learning
  • Designed role-play and case studies for client-relationship skills
  • Benchmarked assessments to industry accreditation standards
  • Tracked cohort data and facilitator feedback to iterate between cohorts

Challenge

The UK's push toward heat pump adoption requires a large workforce of qualified installers, but industry standards and certification requirements are evolving rapidly. The curriculum needed to keep pace with regulatory change while delivering practical, hands-on competency.

Approach

  • Tracked emerging UK energy policy and industry standards to keep content current
  • Managed scoping and sequencing aligned with certification requirements
  • Developed objectives and assessments with industry subject-matter experts
  • Integrated practical workshop components with online modules
  • Built modular frameworks enabling rapid updates as standards shifted
